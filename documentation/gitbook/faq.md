# FAQ

Answers to commonly asked questions about birdr.

---

## General

### What is birdr?

birdr is a mobile app that lets you photograph birds, identify them using AI, and collect them as digital cards in your own personal Pokédex. Track your streaks, earn achievements, and explore what birds are in your area.

### Is birdr free?

Yes. birdr is free to download and use. Free users get a limited number of bird identifications per day. You can upgrade to a paid subscription for unlimited identifications.

### What devices does birdr support?

birdr is available on iOS and Android.

---

## Identification

### How does bird identification work?

When you take a photo, birdr sends the image to an AI-powered vision system that analyzes the bird's features and cross-references your location and time of year to identify the species. See [How Identification Works](how-identification-works.md) for the full breakdown.

### How accurate is the identification?

Our AI is highly capable but not infallible. Accuracy depends on photo quality, lighting, and how clearly the bird's features are visible. birdr will let you know when it's not confident enough to make a reliable identification.

### Why couldn't birdr identify my bird?

Common reasons include:

- The photo is blurry or out of focus.
- The bird is too far away or too small in the frame.
- Heavy shadows or backlighting obscure the bird's features.
- The bird is partially hidden behind an object.

Try taking another photo with better framing and lighting.

### Does birdr work with photos from my camera roll?

You can import existing photos from your photo library for identification.

## Collection & Cards

### What are bird cards?

Each bird you identify becomes a collectible card in your collection. The card features your photo, the species name, conservation status, and details about the bird.

### Can I update a card's photo?

Your card's photo is always your first sighting of that species. Subsequent sightings of the same species add to your sighting count but don't replace the original photo.

### What do the card border colors mean?

Card border colors reflect the bird's IUCN conservation status:

| Color | Status |
|-------|--------|
| Gold | Least Concern |
| Orange | Near Threatened |
| Deep Orange | Vulnerable |
| Red | Endangered |
| Dark Red | Critically Endangered |

## Streaks & Achievements

### How do streaks work?

Capture at least one bird per day to keep your streak going. Miss a day and it resets to zero. See [Streaks & Achievements](streaks-and-achievements.md) for more details.

### Can I recover a lost streak?

No. Streaks cannot be recovered once broken. But you can always start a new one!

---

## Account & Data

### How do I delete my account?

Go to **Profile > Delete Account**. This permanently removes all your data, including photos, sightings, streaks, and achievements. This action cannot be undone.

### What data does birdr collect?

birdr collects your photos (when you confirm a sighting), your location (when you capture a bird), and anonymous usage analytics. See our [Privacy Policy](privacy-policy.md) for full details.

### Can I export my data?

Data export is not currently available. This may be added in a future update.

---

## Subscriptions

### How do I cancel my subscription?

Manage your subscription through your device's app store settings (Apple App Store or Google Play Store). See [Subscriptions](subscriptions.md) for step-by-step instructions.

### Do I lose my collection if I cancel?

No. Your bird cards, sightings, streaks, and achievements are yours to keep. You'll return to the free tier's daily identification limit.
