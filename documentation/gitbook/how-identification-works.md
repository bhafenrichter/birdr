# How Bird Identification Works

birdr uses AI-powered image recognition to identify the birds in your photos. Here's a step-by-step breakdown of what happens when you capture a bird.

---

## Step 1: Take a Photo

Open the camera from the Capture tab and photograph the bird. For best results:

- Get as close as safely possible.
- Make sure the bird is clearly visible and in focus.
- Try to capture distinguishing features like the head, beak, and body markings.
- Good lighting makes a big difference — natural daylight works best.

## Step 2: AI Analysis

Once you take the photo, birdr sends the image to our AI identification service. The AI analyzes the bird's physical features — size, shape, color patterns, beak type, and more — to determine the species.

Your location is also used as context. Certain species are more likely to appear in specific regions and seasons, which helps the AI make a more accurate identification.

## Step 3: Confirmation

Based on the AI's confidence level, one of three things happens:

- **High confidence** — The AI is very confident in its identification. The species is presented to you for confirmation.
- **Multiple matches** — The AI narrows it down to a few likely species. You'll see the top candidates and can pick the correct one.
- **Low confidence** — The AI couldn't make a reliable identification. You'll be asked to try again with a clearer photo.

## Step 4: Card Creation

Once you confirm the species, birdr creates your collectible card:

1. Your photo is uploaded and becomes the card's hero image.
2. The sighting is recorded with the date, time, and location.
3. Your card is added to your collection.
4. A **card reveal animation** plays — the rarity of the bird determines how dramatic the reveal is.

## Step 5: Collection Updates

After a successful capture, birdr automatically updates:

- **Your collection** — the new card appears in your Collection tab.
- **Your streak** — consecutive days of birding keeps your streak alive.
- **Your achievements** — progress toward milestones like spotting your first 10 species or mastering a habitat.

---

## Tips for Better Identifications

- **Photograph the whole bird** when possible, not just a close-up of one feature.
- **Avoid heavy shadows** or backlighting where the bird appears as a silhouette.
- **Stay still** — a sharp, clear photo is easier for the AI to analyze than a blurry one.
- **Multiple angles** help. If the first attempt doesn't work, try capturing the bird from a different angle.

---

## About the AI

birdr uses advanced vision AI to identify birds. While our identification system is highly capable, it is not perfect. Identifications are provided for informational and entertainment purposes. If precise scientific identification is required, we recommend consulting a field guide or local birding expert.
